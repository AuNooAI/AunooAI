Corporate AGI Efforts
Meta, Google, OpenAI continue massive investments in AGI teams and infrastructure, but with more measured messaging about "practical AGI" and "universal assistants" replacing 2023-2024 hype.
Technical Bottlenecks
Scaling limits, costs, and persistent issues with reasoning, memory, and generalization temper near-term AGI optimism. Progress remains incremental despite investment.
Market Correction Signals
Investor skepticism grows as costs soar and returns diminish. "AI bubble" warnings mount, though most coverage expects continued corporate commitment.
Geopolitical Fragmentation
US, China, and EU pursue sovereign AGI stacks, fragmenting standards and innovation. National security priorities drive separate development paths.
Societal Response
Entry-level job losses accelerate, workforce adaptation intensifies, and calls for global AGI governance increase. Society grapples with ongoing disruption.
Alignment & Safety
Critical incidents with deceptive models and safety failures drive calls for regulatory pauses and new international treaties on AGI development.
Probable (Centerline)
Corporate-led AGI development with sustained investment but cautious realism. Progress is incremental, shaped by technical bottlenecks, market friction, and regulatory response.
Plausible (Alternative Paths)
Extended plateau with market correction, geopolitical fragmentation into sovereign AI programs, or alignment crisis triggering regulatory pause.
Possible (Wild Cards)
Low-probability, high-impact scenarios: sudden breakthrough enabling rapid AGI, catastrophic failure, or major bubble crash with public rejection.
Preferable (Global Collaboration)
Proactive international cooperation on AGI safety, equitable access, and governance. Requires sustained intervention and public engagement.
